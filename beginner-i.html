Beginner I

This plan includes easy-to-follow starting workout routines designed to help you build healthy habits and establish a consistent fitness routine.

About this Plan

This beginner-friendly 4-week fitness plan is all about building a sustainable workout habit. It’s designed to help you get started with simple, adaptable exercises that can be adjusted to suit your body’s needs and your current fitness level. Each week brings new challenges that gradually help you build strength, endurance, and consistency. By the end of the plan, you'll be well on your way to making fitness a regular part of your life!

Tips for Getting the Most Out of This Plan:

  • If it’s too challenging: Start by completing half of each workout and gradually work your way up.
  • If it’s too easy: Increase the intensity by adding more reps or sets to push yourself further.
  • If you struggle with a workout: Repeat the previous week's plan or break down the workout and spread it over extra days.
  • If you’re not building a habit after 4 weeks: Repeat the entire plan and focus on consistency and discipline to turn fitness into a lifelong habit.

Workout

Monday, Wednesday, Friday – Full Body Workout

Goal:

Establish a routine and get comfortable with basic exercises.

Warm-up (5-7 minutes):

  • March in place or light jog
  • Arm circles
  • Side step jacks
  • Torso twists

Workout:

  1. Squats (Strength)
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Alternative: Sit-to-stand (sit on a chair, then stand up)
  2. Modified Push-ups (Strength)
    • Reps: 6-8
    • Alternative: Wall push-ups
  3. Glute Bridges (Strength)
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Alternative: Single-leg glute bridge (each leg)
  4. Plank (on knees) (Core)
    • Duration: 10-15 seconds
    • Alternative: Wall plank or resting on forearms
  5. Jumping Jacks (Cardio)
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Alternative: Step jacks (step out instead of jumping)

Cooldown (5 minutes):

  • Stretch arms, legs, and back

Workout Tips:

  • Focus on getting used to your fasting schedule (12:12).
  • Start with short and simple bodyweight exercises (like squats and push-ups).
  • Focus on establishing consistency, not intensity.

Diet Plan

3 Meals in 12-hour intermittent fasting window

Meals:

  1. Breakfast: A protein-rich meal with healthy fats and fiber (e.g., eggs, avocado, whole grains).
  2. Lunch: Lean protein (chicken, fish, tofu) with a variety of vegetables and whole grains (like quinoa or brown rice).
  3. Dinner: Light protein (like fish or lean meat) with a hearty serving of vegetables (e.g., roasted or steamed).

Eating Tips:

  • Take your time with each bite, and focus on the taste, texture, and smell of your food.
  • Start each meal with a glass of water.
  • Take small bites and chew slowly to aid digestion and prevent overeating.

  4. Plank (on knees) (Core)

    • Alternative: Step jacks (step out instead of jumping)

Cooldown (5 minutes):

  • Stretch arms, legs, and back

  • Increase exercise duration slightly. Add 1-2 extra sets to each exercise.
  • Incorporate a mix of strength and light cardio (like jumping jacks or walking).

Diet Plan

3 Meals in 12-hour intermittent fasting window

Meals:

  1. Breakfast: Fiber-rich options like oatmeal or whole-grain toast paired with fruits or yogurt.
  2. Lunch: Lean protein (chicken or turkey) with raw or cooked vegetables and a healthy fat (like avocado or olive oil).
  3. Dinner: Vegetables with a protein option (tofu, fish, or legumes) and a whole grain like quinoa or brown rice.

Eating Tips:

  • Drink a glass of water 30 minutes before each meal.
  • Focus on how your body feels during meals. Stop when you're 80% full.
  • Aim for at least 20-30 chews per bite to aid digestion and improve satiety.

Workout

Monday, Wednesday, Friday – Full Body Workout

Goal:

Start introducing new exercises and challenging your body.

Warm-up (5-7 minutes):

  • March in place or light jog
  • Arm circles
  • Side step jacks
  • Torso twists

Workout:

  1. Squats (Strength)
    • Reps: 20
    • Alternative: Sit-to-stand (sit on a chair, then stand up)
  2. Modified Push-ups (Strength)
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Alternative: Knee push-ups or wall push-ups
  3. Glute Bridges (Strength)
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Alternative: Single-leg glute bridge (each leg)
  4. Mountain Climbers
    • Reps: 20
    • Alternative: March in place
  5. Plank (on knees) (Core)
    • Duration: 30 seconds
    • Alternative: Wall plank

Cooldown (5 minutes):

  • Stretch arms, legs, and back

Workout Tips:

  • Increase workout intensity by adding resistance (dumbbells or bands) or trying more challenging exercises (e.g., incline push-ups).
  • Aim for at least 3 full-body workouts per week with rest days in between.

Diet Plan

3 Meals in 12-hour intermittent fasting window

Meals:

  1. Breakfast: Protein + healthy fats + fruits (e.g., smoothies with protein powder, chia seeds, and berries).
  2. Lunch: A lean protein source with cooked or raw vegetables and legumes (like beans or lentils).
  3. Dinner: A protein choice with a side of non-starchy vegetables and a small portion of complex carbs (like sweet potatoes or quinoa).

Eating Tips:

  • Start each meal with water, and drink water throughout your eating window.
  • Aim for a relaxed pace when eating, putting your fork down between bites.
  • Try using smaller plates to help control portions and avoid overeating.

Workout

Monday, Wednesday, Friday – Full Body Workout

Goal:

Continue to build strength while improving stamina and endurance.

Warm-up (5-7 minutes):

  • March in place or light jog
  • Arm circles
  • Side step jacks
  • Torso twists

Workout:

  1. Squats (Strength)
    • Reps: 25
    • Alternative: Sit-to-stand (sit on a chair, then stand up)
  2. Modified Push-ups (Strength)
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Alternative: Knee push-ups or wall push-ups
  3. Glute Bridges (Strength)
    • Reps: 20-25
    • Alternative: Single-leg glute bridge (each leg)
  4. Mountain Climbers
    • Reps: 30
    • Alternative: March in place
  5. Plank (on knees) (Core)
    • Duration: 40-45 seconds
    • Alternative: Wall plank

Cooldown (5 minutes):

  • Stretch arms, legs, and back

Workout Tips:

  • Focus on increasing strength with heavier weights or more complex exercises (e.g., lunges or planks).
  • Continue with 3-4 workouts a week and aim to challenge yourself each session.

Diet Plan

3 Meals in 12-hour intermittent fasting window

Meals:

  1. Breakfast: A high-protein option with a mix of healthy fats and fruits (e.g., eggs, avocado, and a small fruit).
  2. Lunch: A lean protein with a salad (mixed greens, nuts, seeds, and olive oil dressing).
  3. Dinner: Protein (chicken, fish, or plant-based) with roasted or steamed vegetables and a small portion of complex carbs.

Eating Tips:

  • Be aware of how the food makes you feel. Pause if you're feeling full, even if there's food left.
  • Chewing your food thoroughly helps with digestion and prevents overeating.
  • Aim to drink at least 8 cups of water throughout the day.
